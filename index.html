Introduction

Learning how to build a PC is already hard enough for the first time. However, as long as you choose the right PC components, and you put everything in the right slot, you’ll do great. Lucky for you, this website can guide you through putting all of those expensive components together. Before long, you'll be the owner of a PC that was made by your own hands. In this guide, we’re going to show you how to plan, ​Purchase and build a PC, step by step.

​Planning - Step 1 Determine Your PC's Use

Before you buy anything, you'll need to know what you plan on using the computer for. Standard PCs which are used for things like browsing or small programs can use older, less expensive parts, while gaming or editing focused computers will need more powerful parts.

​Planning - ​Step 2 Set A Budget

It's easy to start buying attractive parts and go overkill without sticking to a budget, only to realize that you're out of money and don't have all of the necessary components to build your PC. Figure out a soft limit and a hard limit and stay within that range.

​Planning - ​Step 3 Know The Part You Need

Processor - Is the "brain" of your computer.
Motherboard - Serves as an interface between all of your computer's components.
RAM - Random Access Memory. RAM will provide more "workspace" to increase your computer's performance.
Hard drive - Stores data. You can buy a hard drive, or you can switch it for a more expensive solid state drive (SSD) if you want an fast drive.
Power supply - Powers all of your computer's individual components.
Case - For storing and cooling your components.
Graphics card - Used to render images on your computer. Dedicated graphics cards if you plan on gaming or using your computer for intensive editing.
Cooling system - Keeps the inside of your case at a safe temperature.
